    Sofia Coppola Biography

    Sofia Coppola is an American filmmaker and actress known for being the youngest child and the only daughter of The Godfather director Francis Ford Coppola.  She made her film debut as an infant in her father’s acclaimed crime drama film known as The Godfather. She has directed several films and has even won The Academy Award for “The Best Original Screenplay” for the movie Lost in Translation.

    Sofia Coppola Age

    Sofia is 50 years old as of 2021. She was born on 14 May 1971 in New York City, New York, United States. She celebrates her birthday on 14 May every year and her birth sign is Taurus

    Sofia Coppola Height

    Sofia Coppola Education

    Sofia went to St. Helena High School, Napa Valley, California. Later on, she attended Mills College, Oakland, California before proceeding to California Institute of the Arts. She did not complete her education as she dropped out of college To Shoot The Godfather III (1990)

    Sofia Coppola Family

    Sofia was born in New York City, New York. She is the youngest child and the only daughter of Francis Ford Coppola and filmmakers Eleanor Coppola. She has two older brothers named Gian-Carlo Coppola and Roman Coppola 

    Sofia Coppola Husband, Thomas Mars

    Sofia is happily married to French musician Thomas Mars. The pair met during the production of the soundtrack of one of Sofia’s films and started dating in 2005. They tied the knot on 27 August 2011 in Bernalda. The two are together blessed with two daughters namely Romy Croquet, born on 28 November 2006, and Cosima Croquet who was born on 18 May 2010. Sofia also dated director Quentin Tarantino from 2003 until 2005. The two have remained friends since their separation.

    Sofia’s acting career started while she was an infant, as she made background appearances in seven of her father’s films. The best known of these is her appearance in The Godfather where she played the role of the infant Michael Francis Rizzi, in the baptism scene. She also acted in her father’s film The Outsiders. Her first short film was Lick the Star and made her feature film directing debut with The Virgin Suicides
